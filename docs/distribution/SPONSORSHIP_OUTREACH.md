# Sponsorship & Partnership Outreach Guide

This document provides templates and guidance for reaching out to potential sponsors, partners, and grant programs.

---

## Elevator Pitch

> **Project Nimbus** is a free, open-source virtual controller that transforms mouse input into joystick commands, enabling people with mobility disabilities to play games and control applications that require traditional controllers. Built by a developer with a disability, it's designed for the community, by the community.

---

## Key Talking Points

### The Problem
- Many games and applications require physical joysticks or gamepads
- People with mobility impairments often cannot use traditional controllers
- Existing adaptive solutions are expensive ($100-$400+) or require custom hardware
- Software-based alternatives are limited, proprietary, or abandoned

### The Solution
- **Free and open source** — No cost barrier to access
- **Mouse-first design** — Works with any pointing device (mouse, trackball, head tracker, eye gaze)
- **Highly customizable** — Sensitivity curves, deadzones, button modes, custom layouts
- **Multiple profiles** — Flight sim, Xbox gamepad, adaptive platform, fully custom
- **vJoy integration** — Works with any software that accepts joystick input
- **Steam Input compatible** — Play XInput games through Steam

### Impact
- Enables gaming for people who thought they couldn't play
- Supports UAV/drone control for people with disabilities
- Used in rehabilitation and therapy settings
- Completely free — no paywalls, no subscriptions

## Follow-Up Best Practices

1. **Wait 1-2 weeks** before following up on initial outreach
2. **Keep follow-ups brief** — Reference your original email, offer to answer questions
3. **Provide value** — Share a relevant article, user story, or update
4. **Be persistent but respectful** — 2-3 follow-ups maximum
5. **Track everything** — Use a spreadsheet to track outreach, responses, and next steps
